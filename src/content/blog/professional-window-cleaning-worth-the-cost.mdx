### What Drives the Price Up or Down

Several factors push pricing in either direction:

- **Number of windows**: This is the primary pricing factor. More glass means more time.
- **Number of stories**: Second and third-floor windows require specialized equipment like water-fed poles or ladder systems, which adds time and risk.
- **Window type and accessibility**: French panes, skylights, bay windows, and casement windows all take longer than standard single-pane sliders.
- **Interior vs. exterior only**: A full interior and exterior clean costs more than exterior-only, but delivers dramatically better results.
- **Condition of the glass**: First-time cleans or windows with years of neglect take longer due to buildup. Hard water stains in particular require extra treatment steps.
- **Screen cleaning**: Removing, washing, and reinstalling window screens adds to the job but makes a huge difference in the final result.
- **Track and sill cleaning**: The tracks and sills collect dead insects, dust, and moisture - cleaning them prevents mold growth and improves how your windows open and close.

If you have never had your windows professionally cleaned, your first visit may cost slightly more due to the extra work required to remove accumulated grime. After that first deep clean, regular maintenance visits are faster and less expensive.
</Callout>

## The Real Risks of DIY Window Cleaning

Many homeowners assume window cleaning is a straightforward DIY job. For ground-floor windows in easy-to-reach locations, that can be true. But for anything beyond that, the risks start stacking up quickly.

### Safety Risks

The numbers on ladder injuries are sobering. According to the Canada Safety Council, **ladder falls are one of the leading causes of serious injury for homeowners**. Every year, thousands of Canadians end up in emergency rooms after falling from ladders during routine home maintenance.

Here is what makes window cleaning especially dangerous:

- **Wet, slippery surfaces**: You are carrying water and cleaning solution up a ladder. Spills on rungs create fall hazards.
- **Overreaching**: The temptation to lean just a bit further instead of moving the ladder is where most falls happen.
- **Uneven ground**: Many Okanagan properties sit on sloped terrain, making ladder placement unstable.
- **Multi-story homes**: Getting a 24-foot extension ladder properly positioned against a two-story home requires training and experience.

A professional window cleaning visit costs $175. An emergency room visit for a broken wrist costs thousands and weeks of recovery time. The math is straightforward.

### Quality Risks

Beyond safety, DIY window cleaning usually produces inferior results:

- **Streaks and residue**: Household glass cleaners leave behind a film that catches light and actually makes windows look worse in direct sun. Professionals use purified or deionized water that dries completely clean.
- **Scratched glass**: Using the wrong tools or contaminated cloths can permanently scratch window glass. Paper towels, newspapers, and dirty rags all carry this risk.
- **Missed spots**: Without a systematic approach and proper lighting angles, it is easy to leave sections of the window dirty. You often do not notice until the sun hits at a certain angle.
- **Damaged seals**: Improper cleaning techniques around window seals can degrade the weatherstripping and caulking, leading to drafts, moisture intrusion, and higher energy bills.

## How Professional Window Cleaning Works

Understanding what professionals actually do helps explain why the results are so much better than DIY attempts.

### Step 1: Assessment and Preparation

A good window cleaning crew starts by walking the property and assessing every window. They note:

- Window types and sizes
- Accessibility challenges
- Existing damage or hardware issues
- Hard water stains or mineral deposits
- Screen condition

They will also lay down drop cloths inside if doing interior work and move any furniture or decorations away from the windows.

### Step 2: Screen Removal and Cleaning

Window screens collect an astonishing amount of dust, pollen, and debris. The crew removes each screen, washes it with a soft brush and mild detergent, rinses it thoroughly, and sets it to dry. Clean screens alone make a visible difference in how much light enters your home.

### Step 3: Track and Sill Cleaning

Before touching the glass, professionals clean out the window tracks and sills. This is where dead insects, dirt, mold, and moisture collect. A vacuum or stiff brush removes loose debris, followed by a detergent wipe-down. Clean tracks also help your windows slide and lock properly.

### Step 4: Glass Cleaning

This is where professional equipment and technique really matter. Here is the standard process:

1. **Pre-soak**: A strip applicator loaded with professional cleaning solution is used to wet the entire pane and loosen dirt.
2. **Agitation**: For stubborn spots, a scrubber pad or razor blade (held at the correct angle to prevent scratching) removes stuck-on debris.
3. **Squeegee work**: Using a professional brass or stainless steel squeegee, the technician removes all water and solution in overlapping strokes. This is a learned skill - the angle, pressure, and pattern all affect the final result.
4. **Detail wiping**: Edges and corners are wiped with a lint-free cloth to catch any remaining moisture.
5. **Inspection**: Each pane is checked from multiple angles to catch any streaks or missed spots before moving on.

### Step 5: Hard Water Treatment (If Needed)

Hard water stains are a particular problem in the Okanagan due to our mineral-rich water supply. These white, cloudy deposits are calcium and magnesium left behind when water evaporates on the glass.

Professional treatment involves:

- **Mineral deposit removers**: Specialized acidic solutions that dissolve calcium carbonate without damaging the glass.
- **Polishing compounds**: For severe cases, a cerium oxide polishing paste is buffed into the glass to remove etching.
- **Prevention**: After cleaning, some professionals apply a hydrophobic coating that causes water to bead up and roll off, dramatically reducing future mineral buildup.

<Callout type="warning">
Hard water stains that are left untreated for several years can permanently etch into the glass. At that point, the only solution is glass replacement. Regular professional cleaning prevents stains from reaching this point.
</Callout>

### Step 6: Screen Reinstallation and Final Walkthrough

Dry screens are reinstalled, and the crew does a final walkthrough with you to make sure every window meets their standard. Reputable companies will re-clean any window you are not satisfied with on the spot.

### Why Twice Per Year Works for Most Okanagan Homes

The Okanagan's climate creates a specific window cleaning cycle:

**Spring cleaning (April-May):** Winter leaves behind a layer of road grime, salt residue, and mineral deposits from snowmelt. Pollen season adds another layer of film. A spring cleaning clears all of this and maximizes natural light heading into summer.

**Fall cleaning (September-October):** Summer dust, wildfire smoke residue, and insect debris accumulate over the warm months. Cleaning before winter ensures you get maximum light during the shorter days when you need it most. It also lets you inspect seals and hardware before cold weather arrives.

<Callout type="tip">
The best time to schedule your fall window cleaning is after wildfire season ends but before the first frost. In the Okanagan, that sweet spot is usually late September through mid-October.
</Callout>

## The Hidden Benefits of Clean Windows

Beyond the obvious aesthetic improvement, professional window cleaning delivers several benefits that homeowners rarely consider.

### Extended Window Lifespan

Dirt, mineral deposits, and environmental contaminants are mildly abrasive and acidic. Over time, they degrade the glass surface, etch into coatings, and corrode frames and seals. Regular professional cleaning removes these contaminants before they cause permanent damage.

Window replacement costs range from **$400 to $1,200 per window** depending on size and type. Protecting a $15,000-$30,000 investment in your home's windows with $350 per year in cleaning is an excellent return.

### Better Energy Efficiency

Dirty windows reduce solar heat gain in winter - meaning your furnace works harder. A study published in the journal *Energy and Buildings* found that heavily soiled windows can reduce light transmission by up to 40%. In the Okanagan's cold winters, clean windows contribute meaningfully to passive solar heating.

Additionally, professional cleaning lets you catch seal failures early. A foggy window (condensation between double-pane glass) means the seal has failed and the insulating gas has escaped. Catching this early means you can replace one window instead of discovering the problem has spread.

### Improved Indoor Air Quality

Window tracks are breeding grounds for mold, especially in bathrooms, kitchens, and north-facing rooms. The moisture that collects in tracks provides the perfect environment for mold growth, which then releases spores into your indoor air. Professional cleaning removes mold colonies and debris, and clean, dry tracks are far less likely to re-grow mold between cleanings.

### Higher Property Value and Curb Appeal

Real estate agents consistently list clean windows as one of the simplest ways to boost a home's perceived value. During showings, clean windows make rooms feel larger, brighter, and more inviting. The investment pays for itself many times over if you are selling your home.

Even if you are not selling, there is something genuinely satisfying about looking through crystal-clear glass at the Okanagan views. That is why you chose to live here, after all.

## What to Look for in a Window Cleaning Service

If you decide to go professional (and we think you should), here is what separates good companies from the rest:

### Insurance and Liability

Any reputable window cleaning company carries **general liability insurance** and **workers' compensation coverage**. Ask to see proof of both. If an uninsured worker falls off a ladder at your property, you could be held liable.

### Equipment and Technique

Look for companies that use:

- **Purified or deionized water systems**: Leaves zero residue.
- **Professional-grade squeegees**: Brass or stainless steel channels, not plastic.
- **Water-fed pole systems**: For safely cleaning upper-story windows from the ground.
- **Proper safety equipment**: Harnesses, ladder stabilizers, and non-slip footwear.

### Transparent Pricing

Good companies provide clear, upfront pricing based on a window count or home assessment - not vague estimates that balloon on the day of service. Ask whether the quote includes interior and exterior, screens, tracks, and hard water treatment.

### Satisfaction Guarantees

The best window cleaning companies guarantee their work and will re-clean any window you are not happy with. At My Home Plan, satisfaction is guaranteed on every service visit.

<Callout type="tip">
When comparing quotes, make sure you are comparing apples to apples. A lower price that only includes exterior-only cleaning without screens or tracks is not actually a better deal than a comprehensive service that covers everything.
</Callout>
