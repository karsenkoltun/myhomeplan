## What Kelowna Homeowners Actually Spend on Maintenance

Home maintenance costs are one of the most underestimated expenses for Kelowna homeowners. After the mortgage payment, property taxes, and insurance, many homeowners assume there is not much else to budget for. Then the furnace needs servicing, the gutters need cleaning, the lawn needs mowing, the driveway needs plowing, and suddenly the annual maintenance tab is several thousand dollars.

Based on typical service rates across the Kelowna market in 2026, the average homeowner maintaining a standard single-family home spends between $3,500 and $8,000 per year on routine maintenance. This figure does not include major repairs or replacements - it covers only the recurring, preventive maintenance that keeps a home in good condition.

This guide provides the specific cost of every common maintenance service in Kelowna and the broader Central Okanagan, so you can build an accurate annual budget and avoid the surprise expenses that catch unprepared homeowners off guard.

## Outdoor Maintenance Costs

Lawn care is a significant expense for Kelowna homeowners because the growing season runs from April through October - roughly seven months of regular mowing, plus fertilization, irrigation, and seasonal cleanup.

A fully managed landscape with regular professional mowing, fertilization, aeration, pruning, and garden care costs $3,000 to $4,000.

### The 1% Rule in Kelowna

A commonly cited guideline is to budget 1 percent of your home's value per year for maintenance. In Kelowna, where the average single-family home in 2026 is valued at approximately $700,000 to $900,000, that translates to $7,000 to $9,000 per year.

Based on the actual service costs above, this 1 percent guideline is reasonably accurate for comprehensive maintenance of a Kelowna home. Homeowners who spend less than 1 percent are likely deferring some maintenance, which typically costs more in the long run through bigger repairs and faster property degradation.

<Callout type="info">The 1 percent rule is an average. Newer homes (under 10 years old) may need less, while older homes (over 25 years old) often need 1.5 to 2 percent or more as systems age and require more frequent attention or replacement.</Callout>

## How Kelowna Costs Compare to Other Okanagan Communities

Service costs across the Okanagan are relatively consistent, with some variations:

**West Kelowna:** Generally within 5 percent of Kelowna prices. Slightly more for services that require crossing the bridge due to travel time.

**Vernon and Lake Country:** Comparable to Kelowna for most services. Snow removal may cost slightly more due to higher snowfall averages.

**Penticton and Summerland:** Generally 5 to 10 percent lower than Kelowna due to lower cost of living and smaller market. Snow removal costs are lower due to less snowfall.

**Peachland:** Can be 5 to 10 percent higher than Kelowna due to steep terrain (makes lawn care and snow removal more difficult) and travel time from Kelowna-based service providers.

## How to Reduce Your Maintenance Costs Without Cutting Corners

### Prioritize Preventive Maintenance

The biggest money-saving strategy is not skipping services - it is staying ahead of problems. A $200 HVAC tune-up prevents a $3,000 compressor failure. A $250 gutter cleaning prevents $5,000 in foundation damage. Preventive maintenance costs a fraction of emergency repairs.

### DIY What You Safely Can

Tasks like changing furnace filters, testing smoke detectors, cleaning dryer vents, basic caulking, and simple landscape maintenance are safely done by most homeowners. Save your professional budget for tasks that require specialized equipment, training, or safety precautions.

### Bundle and Commit

Providers who know they have your business for the season or the year will often offer better per-visit rates than one-off bookings. Annual contracts for lawn care, snow removal, and cleaning typically cost 10 to 20 percent less than paying per visit.

### Schedule Off-Peak

Some services cost less when booked during off-peak periods. HVAC service in September is often cheaper than in November. Pressure washing in May may be cheaper than in July. Where timing flexibility exists, booking early or late in the season can save money.
